UE1 - Administer

Administering Networks and the Internet

This unit focuses on the "Networking" part of the program, including the basics of setting up and managing simple information systems, including the administration of Windows and Linux (Debian) operating systems. During this unit, I discovered the use of simple network equipment such as routers or switches through practical work on Cisco Packet Tracer, GNS3, or on physical hardware, as well as VLAN configuration. I also learned to use various protocols like ARP, ICMP, DHCP, RIP, and static routing.

Essential Components

  • CE1.01 Choose the appropriate network solutions and technologies.
  • CE1.02 Comply with the fundamental principles of IT security.
  • CE1.03 Use a rigorous approach to troubleshoot malfunctions.

Critical Learnings (BUT1)

  • AC11.01 Master the fundamental laws of electricity.
  • AC11.02 Understand the architecture and foundations of digital systems.
  • AC11.03 Configure the basic functions of the local network.
  • AC11.04 Master the fundamental principles of operating systems.
  • AC11.05 Identify local network malfunctions.
  • AC11.06 Install a client workstation and explain the procedure.

Reflective Analysis

What I did well

Having arrived mid-year, I was able to understand some concepts from the first semester on my own using practical exercises (TP) and gradually catch up with the curriculum. In particular, I familiarized myself with basic Linux administration (command lines) and Windows workstation configuration. I was able to catch up on certain concepts, especially through group work.

What I need to improve

During practical sessions involving setting up a network, like in SAE2.01, I had more difficulty using software like Packet Tracer or GNS3. The sometimes temperamental nature of emulation and managing extended topologies required more adaptation time.

UE2 - Connect

Connecting Businesses and Users

This unit focuses on the "Telecommunications" part of the program, which means setting up transmission media and systems, provisioning access equipment and wireless access, such as IP telephony (using Linphone). It particularly relies on electronics and mathematics.

Essential Components

  • CE2.01 Communicate with the client and involved stakeholders.
  • CE2.02 Demonstrate a scientific approach.
  • CE2.03 Choose appropriate solutions and technologies.

Critical Learnings (BUT1)

  • AC12.01 Measure, analyze, and comment on signals.
  • AC12.02 Characterize basic transmission systems.
  • AC12.03 Deploy transmission media.
  • AC12.04 Connect ToIP systems.
  • AC12.05 Communicate with a third party and adapt one's speech.

Reflective Analysis

What I did well

Coming from a background focused primarily on mathematics and physics, I didn't have much trouble catching up with the curriculum in these subjects. Understanding signals and mathematical modeling was a strong point.

What I need to improve

Having no prior experience in electronics, I had a harder time understanding the principles of certain transmission systems and using software like Tinkercad during practicals and projects (SAE), or getting familiar with how Arduino (ESP32) boards work.

S2 Average: 14.34/20
UE3 - Program

Creating IT Tools and Applications

This unit focuses on the design, deployment, and maintenance of a company's information system and the development of IT tools. It covers programming concepts with Python (object-oriented programming or developing dynamic websites using the Django framework).

Essential Components

  • CE3.01 Listen to the client's needs.
  • CE3.02 Document the work done.
  • CE3.03 Use digital tools wisely.
  • CE3.04 Choose appropriate development tools.

Critical Learnings (BUT1)

  • AC13.01 Use an IT system and its tools.
  • AC13.02 Read, execute, debug, and modify a program.
  • AC13.03 Translate an algorithm into a given language.
  • AC13.04 Understand the architecture and technologies of a website.
  • AC13.05 Choose appropriate data management mechanisms.
  • AC13.06 Integrate into a collaborative environment.

Reflective Analysis

What I did well

Being already familiar with Python development, I was able to easily grasp the first-year concepts, especially regarding the implementation of object-oriented programming in my projects.

What I need to improve

Some concepts in web development, particularly understanding the MVT architecture of Django and the link between views and templates.

First Year Summary

During my first year, I discovered various concepts in computer networks (Cisco hardware, switch and router configuration), in telecommunications (transmission systems and ToIP), as well as in software development (Python programming and the Django framework). Having arrived in the second semester, I managed to catch up on the essential knowledge to successfully complete my year. I enjoyed joining this program because it relies heavily on the practical application of knowledge during practical works and SAEs (Learning and Evaluation Situations), which also fosters teamwork.
